Technical blog 5 — Problem Solving

Tell your non-tech friend a story about a time you:
1. Got blocked on a simple problem
2. Solved a problem in an elegant way

  • What was the problem?

    • This particular problem I remember from sprint-3 was when trying to make my website responsive. In the mobile version of my website, I wanted to have my navigation bar to become shorter and place on top of the contents; the navigation bar is set to three-columns wide (based on a twelve-columns grids) and stays on the left side of the webpage in desktop version, and blog contents on the right. When the screen size reduces, I wanted the blog contents to go beneath the navigation bar. I went through a lot of trials and errors before getting to act right.

  • What problem solving techniques did you use?

    • A lot of trying and googling but none worked. I met some of the people from my cohort that week at EDA, so I took the chance to ask for help from my peers. The problem was solved straightaway and helped me understand why my previous trials don’t work.

  • How did you feel throughout the process?

    • At first, I felt frustrated that I couldn’t figure the problem. Then, I realised if I had asked for help much earlier, it would’ve saved me a lot time and to realise my blindspots.

  • What did you learn?

    • Don’t be stubborn. Ask for help when you are really stuck.
